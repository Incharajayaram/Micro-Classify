Team Name: RadarVision

Abstract

Protective measures against small unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) are crucial for national security. The significance of surveillance systems that can automatically identify and classify low radar cross-section (RCS) aerial targets has become increasingly evident. This project leverages a continuous wave (CW) radar operating at X-band (10 GHz) to build a diversified dataset. The dataset comprises 4,849 micro-Doppler signature images of six different small aerial targets, which are used to train a deep convolutional neural network (DCNN) based on transfer learning.

Model and Data set

We utilized the VGG16 and VGG19 models, pre-trained on the ImageNet dataset, as feature extractors to develop a highly accurate classification system. The models achieved classification accuracies of 96.3% and 96.7%, respectively, with minimal false positives and negatives. We also built a Custom CNN Model of 15-convolutional layers, which achieved an accuracy of 96.5% in 27 mins of computing time.

Classes of Aerial Targets

The targets classified in this project include:

1.Three-Long-Blade-Rotor

2.Three-Short-Blade Rotor

3.Bird

4.Bird + Mini-Helicopter

5.Drone

6.RC Plane

Input and Output

On this platform, users can upload spectrogram images, which are processed by our trained DCNN model. The system will then classify the input image into one of the six target classes listed above. The output includes the predicted class and the confidence level of the prediction.

Why this Matters?

As small UAVs become more common, the ability to automatically detect and classify these targets is essential for ensuring security and safety. Our approach not only demonstrates high accuracy but also provides a robust solution for real-time identification of multiple aerial targets operating simultaneously.

Experience the Classifier

You can test the classifier by uploading a spectrogram image of a potential aerial target. The system will analyze the image and provide you with the most likely classification, helping to identify potential threats quickly and efficiently.
